National Survey on the State of Volunteerism in T&T

The Volunteer Center of Trinidad and Tobago (VCTT) challenges the average Trinidad and Tobago citizen to and in touching lives and shifting minds. It acknowledges that people themselves are the most important asset for their own progress, which can only be sustained through their own leadership and ownership of the process.

As a key volunteer coordinating agency in Trinidad and Tobago VCTT recognizes that there is minimal understanding of the contributions of volunteerism to Trinidad and Tobago’s development since independence, little consensus as to who is a volunteer, and how this critical sector is evolving to support Sustainable Development Goals and Agenda 2030.

The contributions are significant; however, data collection and mapping have been ad hoc or even non-existent resulting in the inability of the Voluntary Sector to effectively leverage volunteers as a sustainable resource. VCTT proposes that a national survey on the state of volunteerism in Trinidad & Tobago is needed to provide a qualitative and quantitative understanding of the current state of volunteerism and the opportunities to be harnessed for the development of a sustainable framework for the growth of the voluntary sector.

The survey is to be carried out in three (3) phases.

  1. Online Survey (Completed)
  2. Focus Groups/ Fora with CSOs/ NGOs/CBOs/VIOs
  3. Field Survey

For Phase 1 (VCTT) with the support of UNV and the Caribbean Research Cluster for Population and Sustainable Development, conducted the online survey between the period November to December 2016.

The online survey investigates the following:

  1. Who volunteers? The demographic profile of volunteers in Trinidad and Tobago.
  2. How do people volunteer? Activities and trends in volunteering.
  3. Why do people volunteer? Motivations for volunteering. The ratio of formal volunteers to informal volunteers
  4. Why don’t people volunteer? Reasons for not volunteering.
  5. How much time do persons contribute to volunteering?
  6. The formal and informal experience of volunteers.
  7. Future volunteering and Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
