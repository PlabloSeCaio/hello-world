Why VolunteerBy Lindy-Ann Edwards-Alleyne

Surprising fact: googling the phrase “why volunteer” yields an innumerable “top x number reasons” lists. Giving freely of one’s time and services, for no financial gain, to the benefit of others will probably be “trending” for a very long time. There’s a “why” to fit each unique personality and lifestyle and to help you find yours, see a helpful list below (not a top anything, just a plain old list) of reasons to make volunteering a fixture on your life calendar:

Practical matters: There’s a proven connection between volunteering and getting that next job/promotion

Have day job as a bank teller but you’d really like to work with children? Volunteering offers the chance to step into a role you always wanted to try out, developing that skill to perfection or at least comfortable competence . What about those of us trying to break into the job market for the first time, or, get back into it after a long period away? Volunteering can offer multiple opportunities to add to or develop your skills set . The evidence that there is “a connection between helping out and getting hired” is significant and offers job hunters and day dreamers alike comforting proof that volunteering pays dividends.

It can do wonders for your health

The United States Corporation for National and Community Service study “The Health Benefits of Volunteering: A Review of Recent Research” states that “research demonstrates that volunteering leads to better health” , even articulating data suggesting that one way to stave off poor health later on in life, would be to volunteer . The psychological benefits of volunteering , its link to finding that elusive “sense of purpose” human beings all seek, is an added advantage to young and “grown folks” alike.

Volunteering can set the stage for deeper, more meaningful networking relationships.

The digital age has created endless platforms for human beings to create and maintain connections. Relationships formed through volunteering not only expand one’s social and professional networks, but also provide opportunities to discover and understand other perspectives. Technology has facilitated the meeting of minds and movement of people , bringing words like “voluntourism” and “experteering” into our vocabulary while offering endless opportunities.

Find out “why” and make a deeper impact.

NGOs and philanthropic foundations are implementing programmes and projects regularly, aiming to provide the best support possible for at-risk communities and individuals. Volunteering to be part of these types of activities may offer a front row seat to in depth knowledge about the genuine needs and challenges being experienced by local stakeholders. For the volunteer interested in sustainable impact, the search for the “what” and “whys” that forms the basis for evidence-based solutions can be the greatest motivator.

…and of course, the mushy stuff

No list on the benefits of giving of time and services would be complete without reference to the possible and oft-cited link between volunteering and higher levels of happiness

The best way to find yourself is to lose yourself in the service of others — Mahatma Ghandi
Service to others is the rent you pay for your room here on earth — Muhammad Ali

The decision to volunteer is first and foremost a choice; a proactive way to discover one’s passion, serve and support others and ultimately improve your community and society. The decision to volunteer, whatever your motivation, can offer tremendous benefits, both personal and otherwise. The “why” that drives you might be on this list or might not. The first and best step is to find it.
