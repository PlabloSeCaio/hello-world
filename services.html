FAQs

What are the Sustainable Development Goals?

On September 25th 2015, 193 countries adopted a set of 17 goals to end poverty, protect the planet, and pursue prosperity for all as part of a new sustainable development agenda. Each goal has specific targets to be achieved over the next 13 years

Follow this link from the United Nations for further information on these goals

What’s the link between volunteering and the Sustainable Development Goals?

By its very nature, volunteerism is an important vehicle for sustainable development. Volunteerism lets people and communities participate in their own growth. Through volunteering, citizens build their resilience, enhance their knowledge base and gain a sense of responsibility for their own community.

As we seek to build capacities and to help this global strategy to take root, volunteerism can be a powerful and cross-cutting means of implementation. Volunteerism can help to expand and mobilize constituencies, and to engage people in national planning and implementation for the Sustainable Development Goals. And volunteer agencies like VCTT can assist by providing new spaces of interaction between governments and people for concrete and scalable actions.

How do we serve you?

VCTT is focused on recruiting projects for our volunteers that fulfill the following criteria:

  • Aligns with one or more of the Sustainable Development Goals
  • Addresses or challenges current systems
  • Delivers sustainable results for people and the planet

What will this process entail?

VCTT collaborates closely with partners through a custom built volunteer assessment process to define and understand their volunteer needs, thereafter agreeing on critical areas for volunteer support and interventions. While we understand your volunteer needs may be exhaustive, VCTT commits to working with you on the areas of deepest need and potential for high impact.
